# Tibhar Hybrid K3 FX

## 1. One-Line Summary & Overall Review

* **One-Line Definition:** "Perfectly taming the power of a wild hybrid rubber with a soft, controllable touch."
* **Overall Character:** Balance / Spin-Oriented Balance
* **Overall Score:** **8.8** / 10
> While the original K3 is for advanced players looking for a "knockout punch," the K3 FX is a masterpiece optimized for intermediate to advanced club players who value consistency and connection.



## 2. Key Spec Data

* **Hardness:** **48.0°** (ESN Standard)
* *Note: Significantly softer than the original K3 (53°), reducing the hardness by 5 degrees.*


* **Weight:** **Medium / Average** (Estimated approx. 46~48g after cutting)
* *It is not heavy for a sticky rubber, making it suitable for use on both sides.*


* **Recommended Thickness:** MAX (2.1mm~2.2mm)

## 3. Performance Analysis (0~10 Scale)

* **Speed:** **8.0** / 10
* The explosive distance has decreased compared to the original K3. However, for club players with average impact, the sponge engages (compresses) more easily, so you may actually feel the ball shoots out more briskly with less effort.


* **Spin:** **9.2** / 10
* This is the reason for this rubber's existence. The sticky topsheet grabs the ball firmly, and the soft sponge wraps around it, creating a heavy spin during loop drives.


* **Control:** **9.5** / 10
* Exceptional. The unique "over-hit suppression" of sticky rubber combined with the "forgiveness" of the soft sponge makes it very easy to place the ball where you want during blocks or pushes.


* **Throw Angle:** **Medium - High**
* The ball travels in a fairly high arc. It allows for bold swings without the fear of the ball hitting the net.



## 4. Actual Feeling

* **Soft & Clicky:**
* Unlike hard Chinese rubbers or the original K3, you feel a clear, crisp "click" the moment the ball hits the racket.


* **Long Dwell Time:**
* The time the ball stays on the rubber is quite long. The expression "catch and shoot" fits perfectly, giving the user a psychological sense of security that they are dominating the ball.



## 5. Pros & Cons

### **Pros**

* **Accessible Hybrid:** You can generate the tricky trajectory typical of sticky rubbers without needing professional-level impact.
* **Short Game Stability:** When executing stops or flicks near the net, the ball doesn't pop up uncontrollably but is kept low and manageable.
* **Pleasant Sound:** It produces a metallic sound that adds a crisp feeling to your shots.

### **Cons**

* **Top-End Power Limit:** When hit with professional-level strong impact, the sponge may **"bottom out"** too quickly, making it feel less powerful than the original K3.
* **Durability:** Due to the nature of sticky topsheets, tackiness can fade quickly if not maintained properly.
* **Smashing Power:** For flat hits (smashing) rather than spin drives, the ball may feel a bit "mushy" or lack piercing power.

## 6. Recommendations & Combinations

* **Recommended Users:**
1. **Backhand Loopers:** Players who want the spin of a sticky rubber on the backhand but find hard rubbers burdensome.
2. **Stability-Oriented Drivers:** Players who prioritize ball placement and rallies over one-shot power.
3. **Hybrid Beginners:** The best introductory rubber for those trying sticky rubbers for the first time.


* **Recommended Position:**
* **Backhand:** Most recommended (Perfect blend of stability and spin).
* **Forehand:** Recommended for players who prioritize control over power, female players, or intermediate learners.


* **Similar Rubbers:**
* **Tibhar Hybrid MK:** Very similar concept to K3 FX, but K3 FX feels slightly more tacky and spin-focused.
* **Yasaka Rakza Z:** A similar hybrid type, but slightly harder than K3 FX.
