# Tibhar Evolution EL-S

## 1. One-Line Summary & Overall Review

> **"A perfect harmony of overwhelming spin and stability; the ultimate balanced rubber for loopers."**

If MX-P symbolizes 'Power', EL-S is a rubber specialized for **'Spin'**. It boasts the longest dwell time in the Evolution series and possesses excellent capability to "grab" the ball exactly as the user intends.

* **Style:** Spin-Oriented Offensive / All-round Attack
* **Overall Score:** **9.2** / 10

## 2. Key Spec Data

* **Hardness:** **43.8° ~ 45°** (ESN Standard)
* *Note: While the sponge hardness is medium, the tension in the topsheet makes the actual feeling closer to a hard 47°.*


* **Weight:** **Slightly Heavy**
* *Weighs about 48~50g after cutting. It is not a lightweight rubber.*


* **Recommended Thickness:** **Max (2.1mm~2.2mm)**
* *Max thickness is highly recommended to maximize spin performance.*



## 3. Performance Analysis (Scale 0~10)

* **Speed:** **[8.5]/10**
* It doesn't shoot out like a bullet (like the MX-P). However, it provides sufficient distance upon impact and offers "honest" speed proportional to the power you put in.


* **Spin:** **[9.8]/10**
* This is the **greatest strength** of this rubber. The topsheet grip is exceptional, creating a fantastic "Kick" effect (where the ball accelerates after bouncing) on thin-contact loops or heavy topspin drives.


* **Control:** **[9.0]/10**
* Anxiety about hitting long (over-missing) is low. Because it holds the ball deep before shooting it out, it is very easy to direct the ball to the desired spot.


* **Throw Angle:** **High**
* It creates a high arc that clears the net with plenty of margin. This drastically reduces net errors.



## 4. Actual Feeling

* **Grippy:** You definitely feel the topsheet **"biting/grabbing"** the ball upon contact. There is almost no slippage.
* **Solid rather than Clicky:** Instead of the crisp, high-pitched "click" sound typical of German rubbers, it has a strong **Solid** sensation, feeling heavy and dense.
* **Long Dwell Time:** The ball stays on the rubber for a noticeable moment during impact, giving you the psychological leisure to complete your swing.

## 5. Pros & Cons

### **Pros**

* **Excellent Loop Drive:** Lifting heavy backspin (cut balls) is incredibly easy, making it comfortable to seize the initiative.
* **High Forgiveness:** It has great tolerance; even if your impact is slightly off, the spin generation often helps the ball clear the net.
* **Cost-Performance:** It delivers spin performance similar to the Tenergy 05 but at a much more accessible price point.

### **Cons**

* **Shrinkage:** A chronic issue with the Evolution series. The rubber tends to shrink over time after gluing, so it might not fit the blade if you try to re-attach it later.
* **Weight:** Attaching it to both sides can make the racket quite heavy, which might be burdensome if you lack wrist/arm strength.
* **Spin Sensitivity:** Because the grip is so good, it is also sensitive to the opponent's spin, requiring careful angle adjustment during service reception.

## 6. Recommendations & Combinations

### **Recommended Users**

* **Style:** **Loop/Drive Oriented Players** (Players who focus on rallies, connection, and heavy spin).
* **Level:** From Intermediate to Advanced. (Beginners might find it too sensitive to incoming spin).

### **Recommended Position**

* **Backhand:** For advanced players with good impact, this is one of the best choices for generating stable backhand spin (Great for Chiquita and BH loops).
* **Forehand:** Recommended for intermediate players who value **consistency and placement** over raw power.

### **Similar Rubbers**

* **Butterfly Tenergy 05:** The high-end rubber that EL-S most closely resembles in terms of concept.
* **Xiom Vega X:** Similar spin-oriented feel in the "best bang for your buck" category.
* **Nittaku Fastarc G-1:** A direct competitor in terms of firm feel and high spin capabilities.

---

**A Word from the Coach:**
EL-S is a rubber that can provide an immediate solution for players wondering, "Why do my loops keep hitting the net?" I strongly recommend it to those who want to **dominate the rally and reduce unforced errors** rather than seeking a powerful one-shot kill.
